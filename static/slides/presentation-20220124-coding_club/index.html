My take: Being able to hack code well requires...

  • Access to hardware
  • Access to software (open source = sorted)
  • Access to knowledge (interwebs+OER = sorted)
  • Time to pursue new ideas
  • Access to community

So what is hacking culture?

Not (quite) social engineering. Doing the work to create an open atmosphere which in turn supports allows us all to hack code.

We may have hardware + software, but time + community are much harder to come by

Why hack organisational culture?

Open isn't just a "technology" it's a way of life.(more on this later)

What is openness?

“to teach is not to transfer knowledge but to create the possibilities for the production or construction of knowledge” (Freire, 1998, p. 30; cited in Rolfe, 2017)

"a cognitive trait... considered important in relation to divergent thinking and creativity" (Rolfe, 2017, http://www.irrodl.org/index.php/irrodl/article/view/3207/4461)

"sharing information candidly and consistently" (https://opensource.com/open-organization/21/3/open-practices-executive-leaders)

Examples of everyday culture hacking:

  • Attribution: giving credit where credit is due for code, documentation, ideas, etc.
  • Transparency: acknowleding areas where you're still learning, inviting collaborators and new hackers to take risks and reveal their learning edges
  • Creativity: breaking stuff

Important point: culture hacking works at multiple levels: your team, our organisation, across a sector, internationally, etc.

There are a lot of reasons to do "open":

It's more fun

Public benefit

Fame and fortune

"Open" can be about putting your code in a repository but there is also much wider vision for the Open Organisation and Open Management, which is far more impactful and transformative.

Industry is WAY ahead of HE on this: for one example read more about the software company Red Hat runs as an organisation with open values.

The upside of these paradigms is that the Open Organisation is also a "Healthy Organisation", with perceptible impacts on staff engagement, wellbeing, performance, and resilience.
